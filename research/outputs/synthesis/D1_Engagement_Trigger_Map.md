## EXECUTIVE ORIENTATION — WHY THIS MAP EXISTS

The single most consistent finding across every interview in this corpus is that commercial energy managers do not go looking for problems. They are found by them. Phil Combs (Trane Technologies) called it the "tyranny of the urgent." John Culver (AdventHealth) said facilities directors are "so busy with day-to-day emergent issues that keeping the lights on is not their main focus." James Norton (Texas State) said he wants to know about anomalies "right away" — which is exactly what his current system does not deliver.

**The implication for Clear Current is architectural, not just tactical:** a tool that waits for users to log in will fail. The trigger map below identifies the specific moments when energy managers are _forced_ into action — these are the windows when Clear Current must already be present, ideally having surfaced the alert before the buyer even knew to look.

**Why it matters for sales:** Triggers are not random. They cluster by vertical (higher ed budget timing, healthcare capital cycles, QSR summer peak), by season (winter gas purchasing, summer demand peaks, September fiscal-year resets), and by type (reactive bill shock vs. proactive contract renewal vs. regulatory rate case filings). A sales team that maps its outreach calendar to these clusters will catch buyers at the moment of maximum receptivity. A product team that maps its push-notification logic to these same clusters will demonstrate value before the competitor even gets a meeting.
