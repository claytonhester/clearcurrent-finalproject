## SECTION 1: COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE OVERVIEW

### What Is Happening — and Why It Matters for Clear Current

The commercial energy management software market is undergoing a structural transition that is not yet complete — and that incompleteness is Clear Current's window. The market is consolidating around three poles: **enterprise-grade ESMP platforms** (EnergyCAP, WatchWire) built for large portfolios; **AI-native data infrastructure** companies (Arcadia) built for Fortune 500 procurement and reporting teams; and **energy services outsourcers** (ENGIE Impact) that use software as a retention wrapper around human consulting. None of these poles serves the same buyer the same way, and none has built the conversational, AI-native intelligence layer that Clear Current is building.

Venture capital entered the energy management software market aggressively between 2020 and 2023, producing several well-capitalized competitors — most notably Arcadia ($649M raised, last valued at $1.5B at its May 2022 Series E). That capital wave has crested. The 2024 and 2025 rounds are smaller and more strategic. What is now happening is **execution and consolidation**, not new category creation. Arcadia spent 2024–2025 acquiring RPD Energy (February 2025) and spinning out its consumer-facing community solar business as Perch Energy (announced March 2025, closing Q2 2026). WatchWire was acquired by Tango (the corporate real estate software company) in 2023, integrating energy management into a broader facility and lease operations platform. These moves signal that established players are **widening scope** rather than deepening intelligence.

The acquisition activity reveals something important: **the incumbents are buying distribution, not intelligence.** Tango bought WatchWire to bundle energy with real estate operations. ENGIE Impact uses Arcadia's Arc Plug API to collect electronic bill data — outsourcing its own data infrastructure rather than building it. Arcadia acquired Urjanet (May 2022) for its utility data connectivity and RPD Energy (February 2025) for its procurement advisory relationships. None of these moves represent a step-change in the analytical intelligence layer.

**The practical result:** a market with significant capital concentration among incumbents, a 14-month-old enterprise pivot at the best-funded AI-native competitor (Arcadia), and a structural absence of AI-native natural language intelligence in every major product. Clear Current enters into a market where customers are frustrated with their current tools (EnergyCAP G2/Capterra reviews averaged across hundreds of users: "outdated," "glorified spreadsheet," "chargebacks and splits are hard to understand") — but where switching costs are real and IT security gates are genuine. The window is open. It is not permanent.

**Market sizing note:** IBISWorld and Orbis data reviewed in the Synthesis Brief does not produce a single clean TAM figure the team has high confidence in. The CAGR figures cited for commercial energy services vary from 3.9% to 8.3% depending on scope definition. This brief will not manufacture a TAM figure from averaging conflicting estimates. What the corpus does confirm: the higher education segment alone involves 660+ district energy systems concentrated in universities and hospitals (Synthesis Brief, Section 03); the GRESB 2025 reporting cycle attracted 1,002 fund managers assessing 2,382 real estate assets; and commercial electricity expenditure represents a $200B+ annual spend in the U.S. (EIA CBECS, cited in Synthesis Brief). Those anchors are defensible. A precise TAM figure is not available in this corpus.
