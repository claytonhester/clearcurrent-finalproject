Clients who dismissed long-term energy risk for years are now flooding in as energy costs spike. _"We've got clients flooding in saying: you've been telling us these things were critical, and our energy costs are now going through the roof. **Proactive Mode (Structural, Not Seasonal):**

**Structural Drag:**

## THEIR BIGGEST STRUGGLES (verbatim evidence)

**1. Clients acting too late on energy infrastructure**
The core advisory frustration: clients dismissed long-term risk for years and are now in crisis. _"The biggest challenge is that most of our clients were making decisions based on short-term returns. A few years back they were saying, this return doesn't make sense to us, we're not going to do this deal. We were saying, but you don't understand the long-term trend, you need to do it while you have the capital. Now they're coming back saying, we need to do this now, and we're saying, it's going to take five years to stand up."_

**2. Antiquated building stock with no easy retrofit path**
Legacy commercial real estate was built when efficiency didn't matter financially. _"Buildings were built when energy was cheap, so energy efficiency was never a primary design constraint. A building that was 70% efficient was acceptable when energy costs were low. Now that energy costs have escalated sharply, that inefficiency has become a major financial liability."_

**3. Clients trapped in leases with no exit**
Even when clients recognize the problem, they have limited options. _"You're in a seven-year lease — what are you supposed to do? You can either ride it out or invite us in to help negotiate with the landlord and get to a position where you can mitigate the delta."_

**4. Data access and security as the primary barrier for any third-party AI tool**
Jamare identified this as the make-or-break issue for any energy AI product entering the enterprise. _"The biggest challenge for a company like that is getting access to clients' data... many of these higher-end clients do not want a third party knowing how much they're spending on power. They don't want that information in the market. And they're going to be worried about whether an AI company is training models on their data and potentially sharing it with others."_

**5. The enterprise build-vs-buy problem**
At JLL's tier, point solutions face an existential question — not whether they work, but whether they get acquired. _"Sometimes a company like the one you're describing might have something worthwhile, but with a firm like JLL, the enterprise-level discussion usually leads to acquisition — because we're not going to continue paying for something we can just buy."_

**6. Energy auditors as a structural friction point in tool adoption**
Jamare surfaced this unprompted as a real deployment risk. _"If this tool is built to take their job, they're gonna have a feeling about it. If it's built to make their job easier, now you might have a target market."_ The people doing utility bill analysis by hand today are both the potential champions and the potential blockers — entirely dependent on positioning.
