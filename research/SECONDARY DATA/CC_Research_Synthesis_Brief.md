## 01 — What We Set Out to Do

The project mandate was straightforward: help Clear Current Technologies build the GTM intelligence infrastructure it needs to serve its current pilots, make its May 2026 fundraise, and define the product roadmap for a Series A narrative. That meant answering five questions through a combination of primary interviews and secondary research:

- **Who actually buys commercial energy management software**, and what triggers them to look?
- **What is the energy management workflow** throughout the year — and where does it break?
- **Which product capabilities are most valuable** to which personas, and how often do they need them?
- **Where is Clear Current's competitive white space**, and what does the incumbent landscape actually look like from the buyer's perspective?
- **What does the market data say** about the size, structure, and growth of the opportunity?

We pursued those questions through five interviews across three distinct institutional profiles, then used secondary research to validate, quantify, and expand each primary finding. Below is a side-by-side account of what we found and how strongly the two data streams agree.

## 04 — The Five Findings That Should Shape Every Deliverable

These are the insights that appear in both primary and secondary research with enough consistency and specificity to anchor every deliverable we produce. They are not hypotheses — they are validated findings.

**FINDING 1 — The Reactive Buyer is the Norm, Not the Exception**
Every interview confirmed that commercial energy buyers operate reactively. Don Johnson said "even hospitals" run in firefighting mode. Phil Combs described "tyranny of the urgent." EnergyCAP's own 2026 survey found 76% expect utility costs to rise yet remain reactive on solutions. This means Clear Current's product must surface insights proactively — the "Push to User" dashboard layer is not optional. The user won't go looking for what's wrong. It has to find them.

**FINDING 2 — The Dual-Audience Problem Is Structural and Unsolved**
Andee Chamberlain described it from ops: "I care more about demand and usage... bill processing folks care more about the bottom line." Andi Ault confirmed it from finance: same data, entirely different questions. No current tool serves both well from a single platform. This is Clear Current's clearest product differentiation opportunity — not just billing audit, but a platform that gives energy ops and finance different views of the same data without requiring two systems.

**FINDING 3 — Billing Errors Are Legally Documented, Not Just Industry Folklore**
The 80% overpaying statistic cannot be used. But FERC confirmed $108M mischarged at a single utility. Rhode Island's PUC found 83% of internally flagged exceptions were sent to customers anyway. Harvard Law documented the structural asymmetry that makes this persistent. These are government enforcement actions, not vendor surveys. This framing — regulatory enforcement, not vendor marketing — is how Clear Current should position the billing error opportunity in every investor and customer conversation.

**FINDING 4 — Regulatory Intelligence Is Confirmed White Space With a Quantified Business Case**
Phil Combs called it "super powerful" and Andi Ault's budget timing problem is the exact use case — both were primary evidence with no secondary backing at the time. That gap is now closed. Ohio PUC mandates 275-day rate case timelines; AES Ohio's 2024 case resulted in ~9% commercial rate increase landing on customers with minimal advance notice. The PPL Pennsylvania case filed October 2025 has a final decision date of July 1, 2026 — the same day a university's new fiscal year begins. Boston University locks its budget in March, months before final rates are known. Zero competitors offer this feature. The product white space is now fully documented.

**FINDING 5 — Arcadia's Competitive Gap Is Structural, Not Just Strategic**
New intelligence confirms Arcadia has zero documented higher education customers after 14 months of enterprise sales. Their UX is a dashboard and data API — not a natural language interface for energy managers. Arcadia is simultaneously selling new enterprise accounts while migrating existing customers off a legacy Urjanet/Arc platform — job postings confirm this is the Senior PM's primary responsibility. Their attention is divided for 12–24 months. Clear Current can build the higher ed vertical, campus chargeback, and the AI chat interface that Arcadia has explicitly not built — all three during Arcadia's migration distraction window.

**FINDING 6 — Campus Chargeback Is the Trojan Horse into Higher Ed**
EnergyCAP's most important feature for university customers is simultaneously its most complained-about feature. "Chargebacks and splits are hard to understand. I tried to use the help, but it is lacking details." No AI-native competitor offers campus chargeback at all — confirmed by the Arcadia intelligence report. If Clear Current builds it with a better UX, it creates a moat Arcadia cannot cross without rebuilding their product from scratch.

**FINDING 7 — The Identified vs. Realized Gap Is the Single Biggest Commercial Risk**
Marc Spieler's most pointed question: "Have utilities actually issued refunds based on errors Clear Current found, or is the $10M still theoretical?" This is the question every investor will ask in diligence and every prospective customer will ask before signing. A single documented case — error found, utility contacted, credit issued, dollars recovered — is worth more than all the secondary research combined. This needs to come from John and Dan before the May raise.
